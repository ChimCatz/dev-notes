* Course 2: Ask Questions — Module 2

** Data-Driven Decision Making

*** Definition
**** Use data to guide decisions instead of intuition

*** Goal
**** Reduce risk
**** Improve accuracy
**** Support business outcomes

** Types of Data

*** Quantitative
**** Numerical data (e.g., revenue, counts)
**** Used for measurable analysis

*** Qualitative
**** Descriptive data (e.g., feedback, opinions)
**** Used for context and understanding

** Metrics and KPIs

*** Metric
**** A measurable value (e.g., total sales)

*** KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
**** Metric tied to business goals
**** Example:
***** Revenue growth %
***** Customer retention %

** Data vs Intuition

*** Data
**** Objective
**** Scalable
**** Repeatable

*** Intuition
**** Subjective
**** Experience-based
**** Risk of bias

** Asking Better Questions with Data

*** Compare
**** Before vs after
**** This vs that

*** Identify Trends
**** Increase or decrease over time

*** Find Relationships
**** Correlation between variables

** Common Pitfalls

*** Using incomplete data
*** Ignoring bias
*** Misinterpreting correlations
*** Over-relying on small samples

** Example Use Case

*** Business Problem
**** Low sales in a specific month

*** Approach
**** Analyze historical sales data
**** Compare monthly trends
**** Identify anomalies

*** Insight
**** Drop linked to fewer transactions

*** Action
**** Adjust marketing strategy
