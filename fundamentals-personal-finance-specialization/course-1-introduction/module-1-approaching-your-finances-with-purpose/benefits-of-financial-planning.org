* Course Introduction
** Purpose of Course
- Helps manage personal finances effectively
- Designed for all stages (beginner → advanced)
- Focus on real-life application

** Why It Matters
- Financial decisions are more complex today
- Decline of pensions and government support
- Financial education is rarely taught

** Key Benefits
- Better emergency preparedness
- Improved investment returns
- Stronger debt management
- Reduced financial stress

** Course Coverage
- Understand finances (assets, debts, spending)
- Saving (retirement, house, education)
- Debt management
- Investing (disciplined approach)
- Risk management (death, disability, accidents)

** Learning Approach
- Learn then apply immediately
- Financial quiz for baseline and progress tracking


* The Basics of Financial Planning
** Definition
- Assess current state → define goals → take actions
- Covers full financial picture

** Misconceptions
- Not just investing
- Not one-time
- Not only for wealthy
- Not always expensive (DIY → professional)
- Delay increases cost

** Core Principles
- Ongoing process
- Progress > perfection
- Early action has higher impact

** SMART Insight
- Small early savings outperform delayed large savings over time


* Life Cycle of Financial Planning
** General Rules
- Spend less than income
- Invest wisely
- Maintain insurance
- Balance present vs future
- Build consistent habits

** 20s (Foundation Stage)
- Build credit
- Pay debt
- Start retirement savings
- Balance debt, emergency fund, investing

** 30s (Growth Stage)
- Career + family balance
- Focus on protection and long-term planning

** 40s–50s (Peak Stage)
- Increased retirement pressure
- Prioritize personal retirement
- Define retirement goals

** 60s+ (Transition Stage)
- Shift from income to savings
- Plan withdrawals and healthcare

** Universal Rules
- Build small habits early
- Follow consistent system
- Protect against risks (job loss, health, emergencies)
